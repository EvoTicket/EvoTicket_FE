# About EvoTicket

EvoTicket is an online event ticketing platform designed to help event participants, organizers and venue staff manage the ticket lifecycle with greater trust, transparency and convenience.

The platform combines familiar Web2 user experience with technical trust mechanisms such as dynamic QR codes, ticket ownership records, controlled resale, blockchain-based provenance and AI-assisted information support.

## 1. What EvoTicket Does

EvoTicket supports the main steps of an online event ticketing journey:

- Event discovery and event detail viewing.
- Showtime, ticket type or seat selection.
- Temporary ticket holding during checkout.
- Order creation and online payment.
- Digital ticket issuance after payment confirmation.
- Ticket management in the user account.
- Dynamic QR-based check-in.
- In-platform ticket resale and ownership transfer where available.
- AI-assisted search, guidance and user support.

## 2. The Problem EvoTicket Addresses

Digital tickets make purchasing easier, but they do not automatically solve all trust issues. Common risks include copied QR codes, off-platform resale, unclear ticket ownership, payment disputes and difficulty verifying whether a resold ticket is still valid.

EvoTicket is built around a simple principle: a ticket should not only be easy to buy, but also easier to verify, manage, transfer and audit throughout its lifecycle.

## 3. Web2 Experience

Users interact with EvoTicket through a familiar web application. Buyers can browse events, choose tickets, make payments and view their tickets without needing to manage private keys, gas fees or blockchain technical details.

Organizers can create events, manage ticket inventory, track orders, support check-in operations and coordinate with the platform for ticketing-related issues.

## 4. Trust Mechanisms

EvoTicket uses several mechanisms to strengthen trust in the ticket lifecycle:

- Dynamic QR codes to reduce the risk of a static code being copied and reused.
- Ticket state tracking to distinguish active, listed, locked, used, invalid or transferred tickets.
- Controlled resale to reduce unsafe off-platform transfers.
- QR locking during resale listing or reservation where required.
- Technical provenance records to support ownership and transaction traceability.
- Check-in logs and order records to support post-event verification and dispute handling.

## 5. Blockchain and NFT Layer

For tickets that use blockchain features, the blockchain layer is used mainly to support technical identity, provenance and ownership tracking. It is not presented as an investment product, a financial instrument or a guarantee of profit.

The user experience is designed so that buyers can still use the platform normally. Where a custodial wallet or default technical wallet is used, EvoTicket manages the technical layer required for ticket issuance and transfer according to the applicable platform policies.

## 6. AI Assistance

EvoTicket may provide AI-assisted features to help users search for event information, understand ticketing steps, find help articles or receive guided support.

AI responses are informational and should be read together with official event information, order details and the published policies of EvoTicket and the relevant organizer.

## 7. Platform Roles

EvoTicket connects several parties in one ticketing flow:

| Role | Main purpose |
| --- | --- |
| Buyer | Purchases, manages, uses or resells tickets within supported flows. |
| Organizer | Provides event information, ticket inventory, event rules and operational coordination. |
| Checker | Verifies tickets at the venue or event entrance using approved tools. |
| Platform | Provides the technical environment for ticket distribution, payment coordination, ticket records, resale control, check-in and support. |

## 8. Legal and Policy Framework

EvoTicket publishes user-facing policies to explain how the platform handles accounts, transactions, tickets, resale, payments, refunds, personal data, AI chatbot usage and blockchain/NFT functions.

Users should review the relevant policy pages before creating an account, purchasing tickets, listing a ticket for resale, using AI support or registering as an organizer.
