# Frequently Asked Questions

This FAQ provides general guidance for common EvoTicket questions. For binding rules, users should refer to the relevant policy pages and event-specific terms.

## 1. Account and Access

### 1.1. Do I need an account to buy tickets?

EvoTicket may allow users to browse events without an account, but ticket purchase, ticket management, resale and some support functions usually require account login.

### 1.2. What should I do if I lose access to my account?

Use the account recovery flow if available. If you cannot recover the account, contact support and provide evidence that helps EvoTicket verify your identity and ticket ownership.

### 1.3. Can I share my account with another person?

No. Users should not share login credentials, QR codes or ticket access information. Account sharing may create ticket misuse, check-in disputes and security risks.

## 2. Buying Tickets

### 2.1. When is my ticket confirmed?

A ticket is confirmed when EvoTicket receives and records a valid payment confirmation and the ticket is issued in the system. Opening a payment page or scanning a payment QR does not by itself confirm the ticket.

### 2.2. Why is my selected ticket temporarily held?

EvoTicket may temporarily hold selected tickets during checkout to reduce double-selling risk. If payment is not completed within the allowed time, the hold may expire and the ticket may become available again.

### 2.3. Can I cancel a ticket after payment?

EvoTicket does not automatically provide unrestricted self-cancellation after payment confirmation. Refund or cancellation support depends on the Refund and Event Change Policy, event rules, ticket status and applicable law.

## 3. Payment

### 3.1. What payment methods are supported?

Payment methods may vary by event and platform configuration. EvoTicket may integrate with payment service providers, banking transfer flows or payment link/QR flows where available.

### 3.2. I was charged but did not receive a ticket. What should I do?

Contact support with the order code, payment time, payment method and proof of payment. EvoTicket will verify payment records and either issue the ticket or coordinate a resolution according to the Payment Policy and Refund and Event Change Policy.

### 3.3. Can payment gateway fees be refunded?

Fee treatment depends on the payment method, gateway rules, event policy and the reason for refund. EvoTicket will disclose applicable fee rules where required.

## 4. Digital Tickets and Dynamic QR

### 4.1. What is a Dynamic QR code?

A Dynamic QR code is a time-limited or versioned access code used to reduce the risk of a static screenshot being reused. Users should open the ticket directly from their account before check-in.

### 4.2. Why is my QR code locked?

A QR code may be locked when a ticket is listed for resale, reserved by a resale buyer, invalidated, transferred or flagged for security review.

### 4.3. What happens after check-in?

Once a ticket is validly checked in, it is generally treated as used. Used tickets are normally not eligible for refund except where a verified system, organizer or operational error applies.

## 5. Resale

### 5.1. Can I resell my ticket?

Only tickets that meet the event and platform eligibility rules can be listed for resale. Some events may disable resale or restrict resale timing, price or ticket status.

### 5.2. Is there a resale price limit?

EvoTicket may apply a resale price cap to reduce speculation and protect fairness within the platform marketplace.

### 5.3. Are off-platform resale transactions supported?

EvoTicket does not guarantee the safety of off-platform resale transactions and may have limited ability to resolve disputes from such arrangements.

## 6. Refunds and Event Changes

### 6.1. What happens if an event is cancelled?

If an event is cancelled, the applicable refund process depends on the event policy, organizer responsibility, payment records and the Refund and Event Change Policy.

### 6.2. What happens if an event is postponed?

Tickets may remain valid for the new date, or users may be offered another handling option depending on the event announcement and applicable policy.

### 6.3. What if an NFT minting process is delayed?

If the digital ticket has already been validly issued in EvoTicket, a delayed or failed NFT minting process does not automatically mean the ticket is invalid. EvoTicket may retry, correct or provide another technical remedy.

## 7. Blockchain and NFT

### 7.1. Do I need a crypto wallet to buy tickets?

For supported flows, EvoTicket aims to hide blockchain complexity from the user. A default technical or custodial wallet may be used where required by the platform.

### 7.2. Is an EvoTicket NFT an investment product?

No. NFT or blockchain records on EvoTicket are used for technical identification, provenance or ownership tracking. They are not promoted as securities, financial products or profit-making investments.

### 7.3. Can blockchain data always be deleted?

Data recorded on a blockchain may have different persistence characteristics from ordinary off-chain data. EvoTicket aims to minimize personal data on-chain and keep direct personal information off-chain where reasonably possible.

## 8. AI Chatbot

### 8.1. Is the AI chatbot always correct?

No. The AI chatbot provides informational support and may be incomplete or inaccurate. Users should rely on official event details, order status and published policies for final decisions.

### 8.2. Can I upload files to the chatbot?

If file upload or RAG mode is available, EvoTicket may process the uploaded content to generate a response. Users should avoid uploading unnecessary sensitive information.

## 9. Organizer Questions

### 9.1. How can an organizer sell tickets on EvoTicket?

An organizer may need to complete registration, provide verification information, create event details and accept the Organizer Terms before selling tickets.

### 9.2. Who is responsible for event information?

The organizer is responsible for the accuracy, legality and completeness of event information, while EvoTicket provides the technical platform and may review or moderate information according to its policies.
