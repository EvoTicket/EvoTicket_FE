# EvoTicket Help Center

The Help Center explains how to use EvoTicket and where to find the relevant policy pages for ticket purchase, payment, ticket management, resale, check-in, refund support, disputes and AI assistance.

## 1. Buying a Ticket

To purchase a ticket on EvoTicket:

1. Browse or search for an event.
2. Open the event detail page.
3. Select the showtime, ticket type or seat where applicable.
4. Review the ticket price, fees, event information and applicable policies.
5. Confirm the required legal checkboxes.
6. Proceed to payment.
7. Wait for EvoTicket to confirm payment and issue the digital ticket.

A ticket is not considered completed merely because the user has opened a payment page. The transaction is completed only when the payment is confirmed by the platform flow.

## 2. Payment Support

If payment is pending, failed or charged without a ticket being issued, users should prepare:

- Order code or transaction reference.
- Payment time.
- Payment method.
- Screenshot or receipt from the bank/payment application, if available.
- Account email or phone number used on EvoTicket.

For detailed rules, review the Payment Policy and Refund and Event Change Policy.

## 3. Receiving and Managing Tickets

After payment confirmation, tickets are displayed in the user's ticket area. Depending on the event and platform configuration, the ticket may include:

- Event name.
- Showtime and venue.
- Ticket type or seat information.
- Ticket status.
- Dynamic QR code or access token.
- Ownership or provenance information, where available.
- Blockchain transaction information, where applicable.

## 4. Dynamic QR and Check-In

EvoTicket may use dynamic QR codes or time-limited access tokens for check-in. Users should:

- Keep the account secure.
- Avoid sharing screenshots of the QR code.
- Refresh or open the ticket directly from the account before check-in.
- Follow venue staff instructions at the entrance.

A QR code may be locked or refreshed when a ticket is listed for resale, transferred, invalidated or flagged for security review.

## 5. Resale Support

Where resale is enabled for an event, eligible ticket holders may list tickets through the official EvoTicket resale flow. During listing or reservation, the ticket or QR access may be temporarily locked to reduce the risk of parallel use.

Users should not rely on off-platform transfer arrangements. EvoTicket may have limited ability to support disputes arising from transactions outside the official resale flow.

## 6. Refund and Event Change Support

Refund and event change handling may depend on:

- The event status.
- Organizer rules.
- Payment confirmation records.
- Ticket issuance records.
- Ticket usage or check-in status.
- Whether the ticket has been resold.
- Applicable laws and consumer protection rules.

Users should submit support requests as soon as possible and include complete evidence.

## 7. Disputes and Complaints

When submitting a complaint, include:

- Order code.
- Ticket code or event name.
- Description of the problem.
- Relevant screenshots.
- Payment proof, if any.
- Desired resolution.

EvoTicket may use order logs, payment records, ticket status, check-in logs, resale records and blockchain transaction references as technical evidence during review.

## 8. AI Chatbot Support

The AI chatbot can help users search for event information, understand platform flows and find relevant support articles. AI responses are for assistance only and do not replace official event information, policy pages or final decisions from EvoTicket or the relevant organizer.

Do not submit sensitive information to the chatbot unless necessary for the support request.

## 10. Need More Help

If the Help Center does not resolve the issue, contact the official support channel displayed on EvoTicket and provide all relevant transaction details.
