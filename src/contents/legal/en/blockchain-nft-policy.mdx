# Blockchain, NFT and Digital Asset Policy

This Policy explains how EvoTicket uses blockchain and NFT-related mechanisms for ticket identity, provenance, ownership tracking and controlled transfer where such functions are enabled.

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to:

- Tickets associated with blockchain or NFT records.
- Custodial or platform-managed wallet mechanisms.
- Token ID, transaction hash and provenance displays.
- Minting, transfer, withdrawal or synchronization processes.
- Blockchain-related errors, delays or disputes.

## 2. Purpose of Blockchain/NFT Features

EvoTicket may use blockchain or NFT mechanisms to support:

- Unique technical identification of tickets.
- Ownership and transfer history.
- Provenance tracking.
- Controlled resale or transfer workflows.
- Additional technical evidence for dispute review.

These mechanisms are intended to strengthen ticket lifecycle transparency and traceability.

## 3. Not an Investment Product

NFT or blockchain records connected to EvoTicket tickets are not promoted as:

- Securities.
- Financial instruments.
- Investment contracts.
- Deposit products.
- Profit-generating assets.
- Speculative digital assets.

Users should purchase tickets for event attendance rights, not for investment return.

## 4. Relationship Between Ticket and NFT

A digital ticket may be issued in EvoTicket after payment confirmation. Where NFT functionality is enabled, minting may occur after ticket issuance as a technical provenance layer.

A delayed mint does not automatically mean the user's ticket is invalid if the ticket has already been validly issued in EvoTicket. However, EvoTicket should provide correction, retry or other technical support if the issue affects ticket use, ownership display or resale.

## 5. Custodial Wallet

To simplify user experience, EvoTicket may use a custodial wallet, platform-managed wallet or default technical wallet. This allows users to interact with ticketing features without directly managing private keys or gas fees.

Where users are allowed to withdraw NFTs to a personal wallet, they are responsible for providing a correct wallet address and understanding the risks of self-custody.

## 6. Token ID and Transaction Hash

Where blockchain functions are enabled, EvoTicket may display technical information such as:

- Token ID.
- Smart contract address.
- Transaction hash.
- Mint status.
- Transfer status.
- Ownership or provenance references.

These records support technical verification but do not replace all off-chain event, payment, refund or consumer protection records.

## 7. On-Chain and Off-Chain Records

EvoTicket may maintain both off-chain platform records and on-chain blockchain records. Off-chain records may include personal data, order details, ticket status, check-in logs and support records. On-chain records should be minimized and should avoid direct personal identification where reasonably possible.

In case of temporary inconsistency between off-chain and on-chain states, EvoTicket may review records and take corrective steps according to platform rules.

## 8. Minting and Transfer Delays

Blockchain operations may be delayed due to network congestion, wallet configuration, gas requirements, provider issues, smart contract errors or synchronization problems.

EvoTicket may retry, queue, review or correct blockchain-related actions. Users should not assume that every blockchain action is immediate.

## 9. Resale and Transfer

Where resale is enabled, EvoTicket may update the platform ownership record and then process blockchain transfer asynchronously. The timing and success of the on-chain transfer may depend on external infrastructure.

If transfer fails after payment confirmation, EvoTicket may temporarily hold the transaction for review, retry the transfer, correct records, reverse the transaction or apply another appropriate remedy.

## 10. Check-In

Blockchain provenance may support the broader ticket lifecycle. However, check-in may rely on platform ticket status, QR version, synchronized access state, checker authorization and event rules.

EvoTicket does not represent that every check-in scan necessarily performs live on-chain ownership verification.

## 11. Data and Privacy

Blockchain data may be persistent and publicly observable depending on the network. EvoTicket aims to avoid putting directly identifiable personal data on-chain and to keep personal data in controlled off-chain systems where reasonably possible.

Users should review the Privacy Policy for more information.

## 12. User Responsibilities

Users must:

- Use blockchain/NFT features only through official EvoTicket flows.
- Avoid sending NFTs or tokens to unsupported addresses.
- Keep personal wallet credentials secure where withdrawal is allowed.
- Avoid treating ticket NFTs as investment products.
- Report technical inconsistencies promptly.

## 13. Platform Limitations

EvoTicket may not control blockchain network congestion, external wallet failures, public chain indexing delays, third-party RPC issues or user mistakes in self-custody wallets.

EvoTicket remains responsible for taking reasonable corrective steps within the platform's control where a platform-side error affects the user's ticket right.
