# Complaint and Dispute Resolution Mechanism

This Policy explains how EvoTicket receives, classifies, investigates and responds to complaints and disputes related to accounts, orders, payments, tickets, resale, check-in, organizers, AI support and blockchain/NFT records.

## 1. Scope

This mechanism applies to disputes involving:

- Buyer and EvoTicket.
- Buyer and organizer.
- Buyer and resale seller.
- Organizer and EvoTicket.
- Ticket holder and checker or event staff.
- Payment provider-related transaction status.
- Technical records such as QR, ticket status, ownership or blockchain provenance.

## 2. Submission Channels

Users may submit complaints through the official support channels displayed on EvoTicket, such as Help Center forms, support email, in-app support or other official communication methods.

Complaints submitted through unofficial channels may be harder to verify.

## 3. Required Information

A complaint should include:

- User account information.
- Order code or ticket code.
- Event name and showtime.
- Payment evidence, if relevant.
- Description of the issue.
- Screenshots, error messages or supporting documents.
- Desired resolution.
- Contact information for follow-up.

Incomplete information may delay handling.

## 4. Initial Acknowledgment

EvoTicket will record and acknowledge receipt of complaints according to its support workflow and applicable legal requirements. Under Vietnamese consumer protection rules, business organizations must notify consumers of complaint receipt within the legally prescribed period where applicable.

## 5. Classification

EvoTicket may classify complaints into categories such as:

- Account access or identity verification.
- Payment pending, failed or charged-without-ticket cases.
- Ticket issuance or ticket status errors.
- QR or check-in disputes.
- Resale listing, payment or transfer disputes.
- Event cancellation, postponement or material change.
- Organizer information or event quality complaints.
- AI chatbot or help content concerns.
- Personal data or privacy requests.

## 6. Evidence Review

EvoTicket may review evidence including:

- Order records.
- Payment gateway responses and transaction references.
- Ticket issuance and ticket status records.
- QR version, access logs and check-in logs.
- Resale listing and ownership transfer records.
- Organizer event configuration and announcements.
- Support communication history.
- Notification records.
- Blockchain transaction hashes or provenance references where applicable.

## 7. Handling Process

A typical handling process includes:

1. Receipt and classification of the complaint.
2. Request for additional information if needed.
3. Review of platform records and user evidence.
4. Coordination with organizer, payment provider or technical team where relevant.
5. Proposal of a resolution or explanation.
6. Implementation of correction, refund support, ticket correction, access correction or other remedy where applicable.
7. Closure or escalation if the user disagrees.

## 8. Possible Outcomes

Depending on the case, EvoTicket may:

- Confirm that the transaction was valid.
- Correct order or ticket status.
- Reissue or refresh ticket access.
- Unlock or relock a ticket.
- Coordinate refund support.
- Escalate to the organizer or payment provider.
- Suspend suspicious accounts or listings.
- Provide evidence for further dispute resolution.

## 9. Disputes Involving Organizers

Where the dispute relates to event content, schedule, cancellation, venue, program, entry conditions or event quality, EvoTicket may coordinate with the organizer. The organizer remains responsible for event obligations within its control.

## 10. Disputes Involving Payment Providers

Where the dispute relates to payment confirmation, bank transfer, payment reversal or gateway error, EvoTicket may review internal records and coordinate with the payment provider. Bank or payment provider processing time may affect resolution.

## 11. Disputes Involving Resale

For resale disputes, EvoTicket may review listing status, price cap compliance, payment confirmation, seller ownership, buyer ownership, transfer status, QR lock status and any blockchain transfer reference.

Off-platform resale disputes may receive limited support because EvoTicket may not control or verify the transaction outside its system.

## 12. Escalation

If the dispute cannot be resolved through EvoTicket's support process, the parties may consider negotiation, mediation, arbitration or court proceedings according to applicable law.

## 13. Legal References

This mechanism may be interpreted together with:

- Law No. 19/2023/QH15 on Protection of Consumers' Rights.
- Decree No. 55/2024/ND-CP detailing certain consumer protection provisions.
- Law No. 20/2023/QH15 on Electronic Transactions.
- Decree No. 52/2013/ND-CP and Decree No. 85/2021/ND-CP on e-commerce.
- The 2015 Civil Code and the applicable procedural rules where relevant.
