# General Transaction Conditions

These General Transaction Conditions explain how transactions are formed, confirmed, evidenced and handled on EvoTicket. They apply to primary ticket purchases, resale transactions and related payment flows unless an event-specific rule provides otherwise.

## 1. Scope

These Conditions apply to:

- Primary ticket purchases from organizers through EvoTicket.
- Eligible in-platform resale transactions.
- Payment confirmation and ticket issuance flows.
- Transaction evidence and dispute review.
- Order, payment, ticket and resale status handling.

## 2. Information Before Transaction

Before payment, EvoTicket aims to display key transaction information, including:

- Event name and organizer information.
- Showtime, venue and entry conditions.
- Ticket type, seat or access category.
- Ticket price and applicable fees.
- Payment method.
- Refund and event change rules.
- Applicable policy links.
- Any resale or transfer restrictions where applicable.

Users are responsible for reviewing this information before confirming payment.

## 3. Transaction Flow for Primary Tickets

A primary ticket transaction normally follows this flow:

1. The user selects an event, showtime, ticket type or seat.
2. EvoTicket temporarily holds the selected ticket where the hold function is available.
3. The user creates an order.
4. The user completes payment through a supported payment method.
5. EvoTicket receives and records valid payment confirmation.
6. EvoTicket issues the digital ticket or Ticket Asset.
7. Blockchain/NFT minting or provenance processing may occur asynchronously where enabled.

## 4. Transaction Flow for Resale Tickets

A resale transaction normally follows this flow:

1. An eligible ticket holder lists the ticket for resale.
2. EvoTicket checks ticket status and resale eligibility.
3. The ticket or QR access may be locked during listing or reservation.
4. A buyer reserves or purchases the listing.
5. EvoTicket confirms payment.
6. EvoTicket updates ownership records and ticket access status.
7. Blockchain transfer or provenance update may occur asynchronously where enabled.

## 5. Time of Transaction Completion

An order is not completed merely because the user has clicked a payment button, opened a payment page or scanned a payment QR.

A transaction is completed only when EvoTicket receives and records valid payment confirmation and the order is accepted by the platform flow.

## 6. Temporary Ticket Hold and Order Expiry

Where ticket holding is applied, the selected ticket may be reserved for a limited time. If payment is not confirmed within the allowed time, the order may expire and the ticket may be released back to inventory.

If the user is charged after an order has expired or after system inconsistency occurs, EvoTicket will review the payment record and handle the case under the Payment Policy and Refund and Event Change Policy.

## 7. Ticket Issuance and Blockchain Processing

After valid payment confirmation, EvoTicket issues the digital ticket in the system. Where blockchain/NFT features are enabled, minting or transfer may be processed after ticket issuance.

A delayed blockchain update does not automatically invalidate a validly issued ticket, but EvoTicket should provide a technical remedy if the issue affects ticket use, resale or ownership display.

## 8. Transaction Evidence

EvoTicket may use the following records as transaction evidence:

- Order code and order status.
- Payment gateway response and transaction reference.
- Ticket issuance record.
- Ticket status and ownership records.
- Resale listing and reservation history.
- QR/check-in logs.
- Notification and support records.
- Blockchain transaction hashes or provenance records, where applicable.

Users should keep payment receipts, screenshots and order details until the transaction is fully resolved.

## 9. User Responsibilities

Users must:

- Provide accurate information.
- Review event and ticket details before payment.
- Complete payment within the allowed time.
- Avoid sharing QR codes or account credentials.
- Use official resale channels where required.
- Submit complete evidence when raising a complaint.

## 10. Organizer Responsibilities

Organizers must provide accurate and lawful event information, maintain ticket inventory integrity, comply with event obligations and cooperate with EvoTicket in handling payment, refund, event change and dispute cases.

## 11. Platform Responsibilities

EvoTicket provides the transaction environment, records the transaction flow, coordinates with payment providers, issues tickets according to confirmed payment records, supports resale where enabled and preserves relevant technical records for review.

## 12. Error Handling

If a transaction error occurs, EvoTicket may:

- Reconcile payment status with the payment provider.
- Reissue or correct ticket records.
- Release expired holds.
- Retry blockchain-related technical actions.
- Temporarily lock a ticket for investigation.
- Escalate to organizer or payment provider where required.
- Apply refund or correction measures according to the relevant policy.

## 13. Legal References

These Conditions are informed by applicable Vietnamese legal rules, including:

- Law No. 20/2023/QH15 on Electronic Transactions.
- Law No. 19/2023/QH15 on Protection of Consumers' Rights.
- Decree No. 52/2013/ND-CP on e-commerce.
- Decree No. 85/2021/ND-CP amending e-commerce regulations.
- Decree No. 52/2024/ND-CP on non-cash payments.
- The 2015 Civil Code, where applicable.

## 14. Relationship With Other Policies

These Conditions should be read together with the Terms of Use, Ticket Policy, Payment Policy, Refund and Event Change Policy, Resale Policy and Dispute Resolution Policy.
