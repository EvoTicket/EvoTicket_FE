# Organizer Terms

These Organizer Terms apply to organizers who register, publish events, sell tickets, manage ticket inventory, coordinate check-in or receive settlement through EvoTicket.

## 1. Scope

These Terms apply to:

- Organizer registration and verification.
- Event creation and event content management.
- Ticket inventory, pricing and showtime configuration.
- Resale settings and event-specific restrictions.
- Revenue reconciliation and payout.
- Refund, cancellation, postponement and dispute handling.

## 2. Organizer Eligibility

An organizer may be required to provide verification information before publishing events or selling tickets. EvoTicket may approve, reject, suspend or request additional documents based on platform risk review and operational rules.

## 3. Verification Information

EvoTicket may request information such as:

- Organization name and legal/business information.
- Representative or contact person details.
- Business license, authorization or event-related documents.
- Tax information.
- Bank account or payout information.
- Event operation details.
- Supporting documents required for platform review.

The organizer must ensure that all information is accurate, lawful, complete and updated.

## 4. Event Information Responsibility

The organizer is responsible for the accuracy and legality of event information, including:

- Event name and description.
- Venue, date and showtime.
- Performer, speaker, lineup or program information.
- Ticket types, seat map, pricing and availability.
- Entry conditions, age limits or special requirements.
- Event cancellation, postponement and change announcements.
- Images, logos, trademarks and other content uploaded by the organizer.

## 5. Legal Authority and Event Compliance

The organizer is responsible for obtaining and maintaining the necessary approvals, permissions, venue arrangements, safety measures and operational conditions for the event.

EvoTicket provides a ticketing technology platform and does not replace the organizer's legal obligations as the event operator.

## 6. Ticket Inventory and Pricing

The organizer must configure ticket inventory, prices, ticket categories, showtimes and access conditions accurately. The organizer is responsible for ensuring that the ticket inventory offered through EvoTicket does not exceed available event capacity or violate venue restrictions.

## 7. Resale Configuration

Where resale is enabled, the organizer may be subject to platform rules on resale eligibility, price caps, resale timing, organizer royalty or event-specific resale restrictions.

The organizer should not encourage off-platform resale if such resale conflicts with EvoTicket's official resale policy or creates consumer risk.

## 8. Check-In and Event Operation

The organizer must coordinate with authorized checkers or event staff to ensure proper ticket verification at the event. The organizer should cooperate with EvoTicket in handling QR issues, access disputes, venue changes and operational incidents.

## 9. Revenue Reconciliation and Payout

EvoTicket may reconcile ticket revenue, fees, refunds, chargebacks, disputes, payment provider records and settlement conditions before payout.

Payout may be delayed, adjusted or withheld where there are unresolved disputes, suspected fraud, event cancellation, legal compliance issues or missing verification documents.

## 10. Fees

Organizer fees may include platform fees, payment processing fees, service fees, resale-related fees or other agreed charges. Applicable fees should be shown in the organizer interface, agreement or settlement statement where required.

## 11. Cancellation, Postponement and Material Changes

The organizer must notify EvoTicket and affected users promptly if the event is cancelled, postponed or materially changed.

The organizer may be responsible for refund obligations, support cooperation and user communications arising from event cancellation, postponement or material change, according to the Refund and Event Change Policy, event terms and applicable law.

## 12. Complaints and Disputes

The organizer must cooperate with EvoTicket in resolving buyer complaints, payment disputes, ticket validity disputes, check-in incidents and event-related claims.

EvoTicket may request evidence from the organizer, including event approval documents, attendee records, venue confirmations, refund decisions and support responses.

## 13. Prohibited Organizer Conduct

Organizers must not:

- Publish false or misleading event information.
- Sell tickets without authority to organize or distribute the event.
- Overstate event capacity.
- Misrepresent performer, venue, timing or ticket benefits.
- Avoid refund or consumer protection obligations.
- Use EvoTicket for unlawful, fraudulent or infringing activities.
- Interfere with platform payment, ticketing or resale controls.

## 14. Platform Remedies

EvoTicket may:

- Reject or remove an event.
- Suspend ticket sales.
- Hold settlement.
- Restrict organizer access.
- Require additional verification.
- Notify affected users.
- Cooperate with authorities where required.
- Terminate organizer access for serious or repeated violations.
