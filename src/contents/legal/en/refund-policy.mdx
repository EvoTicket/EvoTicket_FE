# Refund, Cancellation, Postponement and Event Change Policy

This Policy explains how EvoTicket handles refund support, payment anomalies, ticket issuance issues, event cancellation, postponement, material event changes, used tickets and resale-related cases.

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to:

- Primary ticket purchases.
- Resale ticket purchases where supported by EvoTicket.
- Payment success without ticket issuance.
- Ticket issuance or access errors.
- Event cancellation, postponement or material changes.
- Checked-in, locked, transferred or resold tickets.

## 2. General Principle

Event tickets are time-sensitive rights linked to a specific event, showtime, venue, ticket category or seat. EvoTicket does not automatically provide unrestricted self-cancellation after payment confirmation.

Refund handling depends on the event policy, organizer responsibility, payment records, ticket status, resale status, check-in status, platform error assessment and applicable law.

## 3. Buyer Change of Mind

Unless the event policy, platform notice or applicable law provides otherwise, a buyer's change of mind after payment confirmation does not automatically create a refund right.

Users should carefully review event information, ticket category, date, venue, price, fees and refund rules before payment.

## 4. Payment Error

A payment error may include:

- The user was charged but the order remains unpaid.
- The payment provider reports success but EvoTicket has not recorded confirmation.
- Payment was made after order expiry.
- Duplicate payment was made.
- Payment amount or payment content does not match the order.

EvoTicket will review payment evidence and provider records, then apply one of the following measures where appropriate:

- Confirm and issue the ticket.
- Correct the order state.
- Coordinate refund or reversal.
- Request additional evidence from the user.
- Escalate to the payment provider.

## 5. Payment Confirmed but Ticket Not Issued

If payment is confirmed but the ticket is not issued due to system error, EvoTicket should prioritize issuing the ticket or providing an equivalent correction where possible.

If the ticket cannot be issued or the event access right cannot be fulfilled, EvoTicket may coordinate refund handling according to the verified case, event rules and applicable law.

## 6. NFT Minting or Provenance Delay

If the digital ticket has already been validly issued in EvoTicket, a delayed or failed NFT minting/provenance process does not automatically make the ticket invalid.

EvoTicket may retry, correct, remint, synchronize or apply another technical remedy. If the technical issue prevents the user from using the ticket, transferring it through the official flow or proving ownership where required by the platform, EvoTicket will review a suitable remedy.

## 7. Event Cancellation

If an event is cancelled and no accepted replacement is provided, eligible buyers should be offered refund handling according to the event notice, organizer responsibility, payment records and applicable law.

EvoTicket may act as the platform contact point for receiving and coordinating refund requests. The organizer may remain responsible for the event obligation and the economic responsibility for ticket value, depending on the organizer agreement and applicable law.

## 8. Event Postponement

If an event is postponed, the organizer and EvoTicket should communicate the new date and available options. Depending on the event policy and the nature of the postponement, tickets may remain valid for the new date or users may be offered a refund support process.

## 9. Material Change to Event

A material change may include significant changes to event date, venue, core program, access conditions or other information that materially affects the user's ability or reason to attend.

Where a material change occurs, EvoTicket may coordinate with the organizer to provide options such as ticket validity for the changed event, refund support or another reasonable remedy.

## 10. Checked-In or Used Tickets

A ticket recorded as validly checked in is generally treated as used and is normally not refundable.

An exception may apply where there is verified evidence of system error, wrongful check-in, operational mistake or another issue attributable to EvoTicket, the organizer or authorized event staff.

## 11. Resale Tickets

Refund handling for resale tickets may differ from primary tickets. Relevant factors include:

- Whether the resale transaction was completed.
- Who is the current lawful holder in EvoTicket.
- Whether the ticket was already checked in.
- Whether payment to the seller has been settled.
- Whether the event was cancelled or materially changed after resale.
- Applicable resale policy and organizer rules.

Where event cancellation or postponement occurs after resale, EvoTicket may use the current lawful ticket holder recorded in the platform as the main contact for attendance rights or refund support, unless applicable law or event rules require a different approach.

## 12. Fees

Refundability of platform fees, payment processing fees, service fees or resale fees depends on the reason for refund, event policy, provider rules and applicable law.

Any non-refundable fee should be disclosed before payment where required.

## 13. Evidence Required

A refund or correction request should include:

- Order code.
- Ticket code or event name.
- Payment evidence.
- User account information.
- Description of the issue.
- Screenshots or error messages where available.

EvoTicket may also review internal order records, payment logs, ticket records, resale history, check-in logs and blockchain references where relevant.

## 14. Processing Time

Refund handling may require verification by EvoTicket, the organizer, the payment provider or the bank. EvoTicket should acknowledge the request, review the evidence and communicate the next step through the official support channel.

Specific refund timing may depend on the payment method, bank processing time, event circumstances and the completeness of evidence.

## 15. Legal References

This Policy may be interpreted together with:

- Law No. 19/2023/QH15 on Protection of Consumers' Rights.
- Law No. 20/2023/QH15 on Electronic Transactions.
- Decree No. 52/2013/ND-CP and Decree No. 85/2021/ND-CP on e-commerce.
- Decree No. 52/2024/ND-CP on non-cash payments.
- The 2015 Civil Code, where applicable.
