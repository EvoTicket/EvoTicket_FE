# Resale and Ticket Transfer Policy

This Policy governs official ticket resale and transfer flows supported by EvoTicket. It is designed to reduce unsafe off-platform transfers, unauthorized resale, duplicated QR usage and unclear ownership.

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to:

- Ticket holders who list eligible tickets for resale.
- Buyers who purchase tickets through the official resale marketplace or flow.
- Resale payment, reservation, QR locking and ownership transfer.
- Resale-related disputes, errors and refund handling.

## 2. Resale Availability

Resale is available only when the event and platform configuration allow it. Some events may disable resale, restrict resale timing, limit ticket categories, apply price caps or impose additional conditions.

EvoTicket may refuse or remove a listing if the ticket does not meet resale eligibility rules.

## 3. Eligibility to List

A ticket may be eligible for resale only if:

- The seller is the lawful holder recorded by EvoTicket.
- The ticket is active and not used.
- The ticket is not cancelled, invalid or under investigation.
- The event allows resale.
- The resale period has not expired.
- The listing price complies with applicable caps or rules.
- The user account is not restricted for fraud or policy violation.

## 4. Price Cap

EvoTicket may apply a resale price cap to reduce speculation and protect marketplace fairness. The cap may be based on event configuration, original ticket price, organizer policy or platform rules.

The price cap is a platform marketplace rule and should not be understood as a statement that all resale pricing matters are fully regulated by a dedicated law for NFT event tickets.

## 5. Listing and QR Locking

When a ticket is listed for resale or reserved by a resale buyer, EvoTicket may lock the ticket's QR/access right. This prevents the seller from using the ticket while it is being offered or transferred.

If the listing is cancelled, expires or fails, EvoTicket may unlock the ticket if the seller remains the lawful holder and the ticket is otherwise valid.

## 6. Reservation Window

A resale listing may be reserved for a limited time during buyer checkout. If the buyer does not complete payment within the allowed time, the reservation may expire and the listing may become available again or return to the seller's control.

## 7. Resale Transaction Completion

A resale transaction is completed only when EvoTicket records valid payment confirmation and updates the ticket ownership or access records according to the platform flow.

If blockchain/NFT transfer is enabled, the on-chain transfer may be processed asynchronously after the platform updates the resale transaction state.

## 8. Seller Responsibilities

The seller must:

- List only tickets lawfully held in their account.
- Not sell or transfer the ticket outside EvoTicket while it is listed.
- Not share QR codes or screenshots with buyers outside the official flow.
- Comply with price caps and event restrictions.
- Cooperate in dispute handling where required.

## 9. Buyer Responsibilities

The resale buyer must:

- Purchase only through the official EvoTicket resale flow.
- Review event information, ticket category, resale price, fees and policies before payment.
- Keep the account secure.
- Report any ticket access issue promptly.

## 10. Fees and Payout

Resale transactions may involve platform fees, payment processing fees, organizer royalty or payout rules. The applicable fees should be disclosed before payment or listing confirmation where required.

Payout to the seller may depend on payment confirmation, dispute status, fraud review, event status and applicable settlement rules.

## 11. Transfer Failure

If payment is confirmed but the ticket transfer fails due to a technical issue, EvoTicket may temporarily hold the transaction for review, retry the transfer, correct ownership records, reverse the transaction or apply another appropriate remedy.

Users should not attempt off-platform settlement while the case is under review.

## 12. Off-Platform Transfers

EvoTicket does not guarantee safety, validity or dispute resolution for transactions arranged outside the official resale flow. Off-platform resale may expose users to fraud, copied QR codes, invalid tickets or unsupported ownership disputes.

## 13. Event Cancellation or Change After Resale

If an event is cancelled, postponed or materially changed after a resale transaction, handling will depend on the Refund and Event Change Policy, the event policy, the current ticket holder recorded by EvoTicket and applicable law.

## 14. Disputes

Resale disputes may involve seller, buyer, organizer, payment provider and platform records. EvoTicket may review listing history, payment confirmation, ownership records, QR state, check-in logs and blockchain transaction references where available.
