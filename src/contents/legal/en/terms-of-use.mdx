# EvoTicket Terms of Use

These Terms of Use govern access to and use of the EvoTicket platform, including account registration, event browsing, ticket purchase, payment, ticket management, resale, Dynamic QR check-in, AI assistance and blockchain/NFT-related functions.

By creating an account, purchasing a ticket, listing a ticket for resale, registering as an organizer or using EvoTicket services, the user agrees to these Terms and the policies referenced in the relevant flow.

## 1. Scope of Application

These Terms apply to:

- Buyers and event participants.
- Organizers using EvoTicket to publish events or sell tickets.
- Checkers and event staff using EvoTicket tools for ticket verification.
- Users interacting with support, AI chatbot or ticket management features.
- Any person accessing the EvoTicket website or application.

Additional event-specific terms may apply. If an event has specific conditions, those conditions should be read together with these Terms.

## 2. Platform Role

EvoTicket provides a technology environment for online event ticket distribution, ticket management, payment coordination, controlled resale, Dynamic QR check-in, technical provenance and user support.

Unless expressly stated for a specific event, EvoTicket is not automatically the organizer of the event. The organizer remains responsible for the event content, venue, timing, program, eligibility conditions and legal ability to conduct the event.

## 3. Definitions

| Term | Meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Buyer | A user who purchases, holds, uses or resells a ticket through EvoTicket. |
| Organizer | A party that registers on EvoTicket to publish events, manage ticket inventory and coordinate event operations. |
| Ticket | A digital right to attend a specific event, showtime, ticket type or seat. |
| Ticket Asset | A platform record representing a digital ticket, its status, owner and technical history. |
| Dynamic QR | A time-limited or versioned QR/access token used for check-in. |
| Resale | An official in-platform process for listing and purchasing eligible tickets from existing holders. |
| NFT | A blockchain-based technical record that may represent ticket identity, provenance or ownership history where enabled. |
| Custodial wallet | A technical wallet managed or facilitated by the platform to support blockchain functions without requiring the user to manage private keys directly. |

## 4. Account Registration and Security

Users must provide accurate information when creating or using an account. Users are responsible for safeguarding login credentials and for all actions conducted through their accounts.

Users must not:

- Share passwords or authentication credentials.
- Share QR codes or ticket access tokens for unauthorized use.
- Use another person's account without permission.
- Use bots, scripts, automation or abusive methods to purchase, hold or resell tickets.
- Circumvent ticket limits, resale rules, check-in rules or platform security measures.

EvoTicket may restrict, suspend or terminate account access where there is suspected fraud, unauthorized access, policy violation, payment risk, resale abuse or harm to the platform, organizers or other users.

## 5. Ticket Purchase and Payment

Ticket purchase follows the transaction flow provided on the platform. A transaction is normally completed through the following steps:

1. Event, showtime, ticket type or seat selection.
2. Temporary ticket hold, where available.
3. Order creation.
4. Payment through the supported payment method.
5. Payment confirmation by EvoTicket's system flow.
6. Ticket issuance in the user account.
7. Optional blockchain/NFT minting or provenance processing where enabled.

Opening a payment page, scanning a payment QR or initiating a payment does not by itself confirm the order. The order is completed only when the platform records a valid payment confirmation.

## 6. Ticket Issuance and Use

After valid payment confirmation, EvoTicket records and issues the ticket in the user's account. Ticket validity depends on the ticket status, event status, payment status, resale status and check-in status shown or recorded by the system.

Users must review event details before purchase, including event name, organizer, venue, date, showtime, ticket type, seat information, price, fees, refund rules, entry conditions and any event-specific restrictions.

## 7. Dynamic QR and Check-In

EvoTicket may use Dynamic QR codes or time-limited access tokens to reduce the risk of static code copying. A QR code may change, expire, be refreshed, be locked or become invalid depending on ticket status and system rules.

Users must not share screenshots, QR codes or access tokens with unauthorized parties. If a validly checked-in ticket is recorded by the system, it is generally treated as used.

## 8. Resale and Transfer

Where resale is enabled, eligible ticket holders may list tickets through the official EvoTicket resale flow. EvoTicket may apply price caps, ticket status checks, QR locking, reservation windows and transfer rules to protect marketplace integrity.

Off-platform resale or transfer arrangements are not guaranteed by EvoTicket. EvoTicket may refuse support for disputes that arise outside the official platform flow, except where required by applicable law or where EvoTicket determines that platform intervention is appropriate.

## 9. Blockchain and NFT Functions

Where enabled, blockchain or NFT functions support technical identification, provenance and ownership tracking. NFT or blockchain records on EvoTicket are not promoted as securities, financial instruments, investment products or profit-generating assets.

Blockchain processing may be asynchronous. A delayed mint, transfer or technical synchronization issue does not automatically invalidate a ticket that has already been validly issued by EvoTicket, unless the issue prevents the user from exercising the ticket right and cannot be reasonably remedied.

## 10. AI Chatbot

The EvoTicket AI chatbot provides informational assistance. It may help users search for events, understand platform flows or find policy information. AI responses are not legal advice, final dispute decisions or binding commitments unless confirmed through an official EvoTicket channel.

Users should not submit unnecessary sensitive information to the chatbot.

## 11. Prohibited Conduct

Users must not:

- Purchase tickets using fraudulent payment methods.
- Use stolen accounts, stolen cards or unauthorized payment information.
- Manipulate ticket holds, queues or inventory.
- Resell tickets outside the permitted flow when the event or platform restricts resale.
- Copy, sell, leak or misuse QR codes.
- Interfere with check-in tools or event staff operations.
- Upload unlawful, infringing or harmful content.
- Use EvoTicket to violate applicable law or third-party rights.

## 12. Organizer Responsibilities

Organizers are responsible for:

- Accurate event descriptions, timing, venue and ticket information.
- Legal authority to organize the event and sell tickets.
- Compliance with venue, safety, tax, consumer and event regulations.
- Handling event cancellation, postponement or material change obligations according to the applicable policy.
- Cooperating with EvoTicket in complaint and dispute handling.

## 13. Platform Rights

EvoTicket may:

- Suspend suspicious accounts or transactions.
- Lock tickets or QR access for security or resale control.
- Request additional verification from users or organizers.
- Correct system errors and reconcile inconsistent payment, ticket, resale or blockchain states.
- Update policies, features and security measures where necessary.

Users may submit complaints through the official support channel. EvoTicket may review order records, payment records, ticket status, QR/check-in logs, resale history, support history and blockchain transaction references where relevant.
