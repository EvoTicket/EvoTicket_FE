# Ticket Policy

This Ticket Policy explains how EvoTicket issues, manages, verifies and handles digital tickets, Dynamic QR codes, check-in records and ticket status changes.

## 1. Scope

This Policy applies to:

- Tickets purchased through EvoTicket.
- Tickets acquired through official in-platform resale.
- Digital ticket records shown in the user's account.
- Dynamic QR codes or access tokens used for check-in.
- Ticket status changes, including active, locked, used, invalid or transferred states.

## 2. Nature of a Ticket

A ticket on EvoTicket represents a digital right to attend a specific event, showtime, ticket type, access category or seat. The right is subject to event rules, venue requirements, platform policies and applicable law.

A ticket is not a general-purpose stored value, payment instrument, security or investment product.

## 3. Ticket Issuance

Tickets are issued after EvoTicket records valid payment confirmation according to the platform transaction flow.

Where blockchain/NFT functionality is enabled, NFT minting or provenance recording may occur after ticket issuance. The validity of event access should be determined primarily by the ticket status and access records in EvoTicket, together with any applicable technical provenance records.

## 4. Ticket Information

A ticket may include:

- Event name.
- Organizer name.
- Venue.
- Date and showtime.
- Ticket type, zone, seat or access category.
- Ticket status.
- QR/access token information.
- Owner/account information shown in the platform.
- Blockchain token ID, transaction hash or provenance information where enabled.

Users must review ticket details promptly after purchase and contact support if any material error is found.

## 5. Ticket Status

Ticket status may include, depending on the platform configuration:

| Status | General meaning |
| --- | --- |
| Active | The ticket is currently valid for use, subject to event timing and entry rules. |
| Pending | The ticket or related technical process is not fully completed. |
| Locked | The ticket or QR access is temporarily restricted, for example during resale listing or investigation. |
| Listed | The ticket is available or reserved in an official resale flow. |
| Transferred | Ownership or ticket control has moved to another user through an approved flow. |
| Used | The ticket has been checked in or recorded as used. |
| Invalid | The ticket is no longer valid due to cancellation, fraud, error correction or other policy reasons. |

## 6. Dynamic QR and Access Tokens

EvoTicket may use Dynamic QR codes, time-limited tokens or versioned access codes. These mechanisms reduce the risk of a static QR code being copied and used by another person.

Users should:

- Open the ticket from the EvoTicket account near the check-in time.
- Avoid relying on old screenshots.
- Avoid sharing the QR code with others.
- Keep the account secure.

A QR code may expire, rotate, be refreshed or be invalidated by system rules.

## 7. QR Locking

EvoTicket may lock QR access or ticket usage in cases such as:

- Ticket listing for resale.
- Resale reservation in progress.
- Ownership transfer.
- Suspected fraud.
- Payment or order review.
- Event cancellation or invalidation.
- Security or operational incidents.

When a ticket is locked, the user may be unable to check in until the lock is removed or the case is resolved.

## 8. Check-In

Check-in may involve verification of QR/access token validity, event and showtime information, ticket status, QR version, checker authorization and local or synchronized ticket access records.

EvoTicket does not represent that every check-in scan necessarily performs live on-chain ownership verification. Blockchain or provenance records may support the broader ticket lifecycle, while check-in decisions may rely on the platform's synchronized access state and operational controls.

## 9. Used Tickets

Once a ticket is validly checked in, it is generally treated as used. Used tickets are normally not eligible for refund, resale or transfer, except where a verified system, organizer or operational error requires correction.

## 10. User Responsibility

Users are responsible for:

- Protecting account credentials.
- Protecting QR codes and access tokens.
- Avoiding unauthorized sharing or resale.
- Checking ticket information before the event.
- Arriving with sufficient time for verification.
- Following event and venue instructions.

## 11. Organizer Responsibility

Organizers are responsible for event entry rules, venue access requirements, event schedule, ticket category descriptions and coordination with check-in staff.

## 12. Platform Responsibility

EvoTicket provides ticket records, QR/access tools, check-in support and technical logs. EvoTicket may correct ticket status or access records where necessary to address payment errors, resale transfers, fraud, event changes or operational incidents.

## 13. Legal References

This Policy may be interpreted together with:

- Law No. 19/2023/QH15 on Protection of Consumers' Rights.
- Law No. 20/2023/QH15 on Electronic Transactions.

- Decree No. 85/2021/ND-CP amending e-commerce regulations.
- Decree No. 13/2023/ND-CP on Personal Data Protection, where ticket and check-in records include personal data.
